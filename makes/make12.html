The image made above shows a possible future where I see AI being used as assistance not as a replacement but just a tool to verify someone's work as they continue on and if there were issues then it would alert the employee and offer a solution to their mistake. I find this as an incredibly realistic outcome of the future where things don't change as much as someone would think apart from the expanse of AI being personally trained assistants for any job to work as checkers for any mistakes made. In my opinion it shows a more advanced view on the present use of AI work as currently most ways AI's are used are either where AI work is made and submitted as it is with known human edit, or slight human changes to match the person's style. In this future AI's are just personally trained for companies to function as a safety net to ensure nothing submitted is incorrect. I purposefully chose this because I personally feel that most companies especially office job based companies wouldn't get rid of all their employees to replace them with, because they wouldn't want to risk the public outlash so it functions as an approvable middle ground of utilising the advance in AI and still having employees work do their jobs.

3. I wouldn't say this future is desirable, as in my personal opinion the use of AI in any workforce feels like a potential danger, however as opposed to alternatives where AI's are fully replacing the human workforce, I would say this is a desirable outcome for AI's future in jobs and companies. I think what this future really shows about the present day, is that no matter what, AI will not replace human labor, but it will become an ally to human work. I think if we want to work towards this future there are multiple steps that people need to take. I think the first step needed to be made is the dissolution of LLMs or large language models, like Google Gemini, and Chat-GPT. This is because frankly LLMs suck for specialized work, since these models utilize general training, and function using information on the internet. The next step would be either companies making their own AI's that are trained specifically for whatever they want their companies to do so that it understands what it's meant to check for in other employees' work.
