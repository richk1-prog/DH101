3. My biggest concern from this research is the sheer environmental cost taken by only training AI's. I was genuinely shocked to learn that 552 tons were released in the training of Chat-GPT version 3. It is incredibly worrying to think about that and know this is probably the case for every AI being developed and updated, not just Chat-GPT. I would like to believe that it will be possible at some point for AI to be environmentally friendly and sustainable, however I won't deny the likelihood of it in my opinion is low. Not just because it would take time to research that kind of thing and so it would take a while till we'd know how, but also because from all I've seen about these companies, they're more focused on making as much money as possible with hardly any true regard for the consequences. The best we can do right now is spread the information about the consequences of these AI data centers and companies, and for us to be decisive with how we use AI's and the frequency in which we use them.
