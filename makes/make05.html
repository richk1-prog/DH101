Make 5

1. I chose a segment from “The King in Yellow” by Robert W. Chambers, from “One by one I studied the well-worn pages…” to the “…sun-warmed sails of the shipping in the harbour.” (2083 words)

2. Voyant, went into the textual details of the excerpt, displaying things like consistent phrasing, and some of the most used terms, which helped me understand key words and key components of the story. However it doesn’t provide any analysis as to why the phrases or, most important words, are actually important to the story.

3. Chat GPT Prompt: “Provide a rhetorical analysis of this excerpt from "the king in yellow": (the copied excerpt)” Chat GPT goes into more of the symbolism, and the importance of the altered perspectives of the characters and of time itself, and the cosmic horror that fills both the excerpt and the entire story. However Chat GPT struggles with not taking outside information to provide its analysis, along with using more of the story than the provided excerpt to ‘improve’ its analysis, and doesn’t actually provide any direct quotation as a basis for its analysis.

4. Reflection Within both analyses of the excerpt it made me see how differently programs interpret and view provided literature. As Voyant merely analyzed repetition of phrases, and unique words throughout the excerpt, but it lacked any clear understanding as to why the phrases and words mattered to the story, only knowing the words didn’t seem common, and were put in key parts of the story, as important parts. In Voyant’s eyes it views the “meaning” at least when referring to texts, as the separation of some words, and those words, connected to them, without disclosing the true purpose behind them. Chat GPT went into analyzing the intent behind the writings, taking in every single detail and providing a brief, few sentences on each one preferring quantity over details. It also pulls information from outside sources without providing any quotes, or evidence that directly supports points. With Chat GPT’s interpretation of “meaning” as literal needing to find the meaning in every detail, and point out each detail even if it comes from knowledge it shouldn’t be able to gauge from just an excerpt, or just the story, but from scanning the internet to help it understand better.
