I ended up using sources I used in our previous make when we made a map of AI companies and the companies they were connected to specifically for this one Time, and the Guardian, who discussed Sama the data labelling company. For me the hardest to find was me trying to determine who was to blame for the poor conditions for the Kenyan workers. Though from what I read Sama and Open AI blame the other for the work conditions, so I think it really just comes down to personal belief or opinion. So while I am not certain whether or not Open AI did or didn't know about the way Sama treated their workers, or if they in fact were underpaying Sama, I do know that Sama at least should have known the work conditions of their own locations, and how much they are paying so either, Sama is run by ignorant idiots, or they knew what they were doing. However I also doubt that Open AI didn't have any preliminary knowledge as to how Sama did their data labelling. I think what surprised me, more horrified me, was when I read an example of one of the things these data labellers have to read, and how genuinely disgusting it was and the fact that these workers had to read things like it multiple times just to label it properly.

When it came to the I already knew I wanted it to be pins with information connecting to whatever the main page was about, so I checked through the templates on Canvas and found one that I could use and had sort of an AI technology theme to it already. My audience really was for anyone, especially anyone who uses AI so they can know what's going on behind the scenes of AI's that are being used daily. And I do want them to experience a similar shock and horror seeing a fragment of the type of things they have to read and see daily. In part to try and make people feel bad but more to draw attention that if people weren't reading these things that exist on the internet, and that every time they use AI part of why it works is because people suffering underpaid, working long hours, and during those hours being subjected to the most heinous things on the internet.
