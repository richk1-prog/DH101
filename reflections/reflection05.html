Week 5 Reflection

Core Question

How does AI alter authorship and remix culture? Who owns AI-made art?

AI changes authorship, as it is no longer about what inspired the artwork, or creation, and more what was the prompt or basis for this work, as AI functions to take information and mix it together to create something "different", and makes authorship non-existent. As for who actually should be able to claim ownership rights for AI art there are three main potential candidates, the AI itself, the prompter, or the AI company, however really none of them should have ownership rights. The prompter only had the idea, they didn't actually do anything but instruct the AI in what they wanted to see, the AI company only made the AI they didn't think of the idea, nor did they create the work, but AI under ownership laws doesn't own the art either, and since most AI works of art are replicable by other AI's ownership would not exist and it falls under free use.
