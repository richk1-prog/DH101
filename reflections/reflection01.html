Week 1 Reflection

Core Question

What counts as "making" in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

In this current age of AI a good way to describe "making" includes, truly thinking about, and holding a form of care into the output, along with having an understanding as to the how and why its being made. When people allow for machines to take part in this "making" then not just is the care for what is being produced lost, but also a part of understanding how it gets made is lost. This comes from the fact that you yourself don't completely understand how it works, because realistically if you did understand how to make it all yourself, you wouldn't have outsourced a part of the making to an AI. However if you did know how to make it yourself but for some reason decided to have an AI contribute instead of doing it yourself, then it shows a strong lack of care for what is being made, if you didn't want to take the time to do something you already could. In exchange for the only thing that is gained from asking AI to help, that being time spent elsewhere because you aren't spending your time working on making.
