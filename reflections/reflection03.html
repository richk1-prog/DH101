Week 3 Reflection

Core Question

What does "authenticity" mean when identity is co-constructed with AI?

Authenticity now in our age with AI co-constructing every detail in life doesn't exist, however theoretically the same can be true before AI, as proper pure authenticity hasn't existed since people could photoshop, and could edit their own videos. The difference between AI and photoshop co-construction is that with photoshop it required some skill and the ability to utilize it to make their own edits and fake images, but with AI the problem is everyone can access it and it requires the skill of "typing words correctly" into an AI software. Authenticity itself in this age can best be considered as viewing anything in the physical world with ones own eyes, not seeing something on your phone, laptop, ect. but actually walking around observing the world we inhabit. Now an authentic identity as a person has nothing to do with AI, but with how we as people interact with our environments, people in our lives, media we consume, and the things we do, AI just so happens to be a part of the media we consume, it is up to us how we let it shape our identity. Either way authentic identities aren't truly real, because our identities are molded by everything else in our lives.
