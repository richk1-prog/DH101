Week 8 Reflection

Core Question

Who is visible/invisible in AI networks? How does visualization reveal or obscure power?

In AI networks for the most part the people who are visible are the obvious, the main company, and its CEO, the main engineers, and coders for it. However what remains invisible, are the locations from which the AI stores information, its, data centers, where and the people who do the training and cheap labor for the company, in order to shield the company's image by having it be public. Visualization itself, works to ensure a control on information, since in most cases if there is no possible way to ever verify a claim ourselves, we are left either believing in a voice, who could lie, so the ability to make important details invisible grants power over in theory, beliefs. and the more that remains hidden the easier, verbal truth, could be brushed off as a rumor or lie.
